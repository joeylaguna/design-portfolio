My multicultural upbringing as a 1.5 generation immigrant and Navy brat has largely shaped who I am today, both as a designer and as a person. My immigrant experience between cultures, growing up in the Philippines, Japan, and the U.S., cultivated empathy within me while witnessing my parents uproot our family every 3-4 years instilled resilience and a strong work ethic. Similar to many creatives, a love for art was the start. I became a designer through sheer will and quiet stubbornness to keep creativity a part of my daily routine. In a sense, I was determined to never grow up.

Continual growth & learning


Kimberly soldering LED lights volunteering at Cymaspace

Winning hackathon team at Re:source Hackathon

After getting a Design degree from UC Davis, I worked as a graphic designer for a couple of years. Most recently, I worked at a translation agency where I helped make election materials and health documents more accessible to non-English speaking demographics and screen-reader users. I felt like I was making a difference, but I wondered how much? Are people actually using these tools? How are they accessing them? What can we do better? My curiosity made me want to deepen my knowledge of who I'm designing for.

For two years, I slowly transitioned into product design, attending conferences to learn about issues surrounding tech and applying my learnings at hackathons. Eventually, I made the decision to pursue UX design in order to find answers to those types of questions that I had before. I yearned to be able to contribute both creatively and analytically to the design process, so I took it upon myself to enroll in Bloc's Design Apprenticeship. Shortly after, I collaborated with a small group of colleagues in establishing a design learning community in Sacramento to help other designers grow. Through UX design, I aspire to serve marginalized and underserved communities to improve their quality of life. It won't be easy, and I'm just getting started.
